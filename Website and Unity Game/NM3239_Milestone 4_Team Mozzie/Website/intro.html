ABOUT DENGUE

The Dengue Situation in Singapore

Dengue fever is a mosquito-borne disease caused by the dengue virus. The virus does not spread directly instead it is spread by several species of mosquito of the Aedes type through their bite. People who are infected with dengue virus showed a wide range of symptoms which include fever, headache, pain behind the eyes, muscle and joint pain as well as rash on their body. In some cases, dengue can be life threatening(severe dengue). The symptoms are abdominal pain, vomiting, breathing difficulty and decreased in blood platelets that can lead to internal bleeding. Dengue is usually not fatal but globally about 2.5% of those who contract severe dengue dies. There is no cure for dengue and contracting it once does not protect you from getting it again. In fact, people who contracted twice have it worse. Thus, the method to control or prevent the transmission of dengue virus is to combat vector mosquitoes.

DENGUE AROUND THE WORLD

Dengue is the fastest-growing mosquito-borne viral infection, and its impact today is 30 times greater than 50 years ago. In the 1970s, less than 10 countries had reported epidemics of dengue. Now dengue is present in over 150 countries. While most reported cases are in Latin America, Southeast Asia, and the Western Pacific. The virus is also now present in many African countries which also include Europe, USA and China.

WHO IS AT RISK

The World Health Organization estimates that 40% of the population is at risk of contracting the virus despite their age.
