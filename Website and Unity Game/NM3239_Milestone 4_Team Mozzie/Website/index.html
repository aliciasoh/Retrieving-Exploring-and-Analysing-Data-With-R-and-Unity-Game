If it was not effective, then are there any correlations between dengue incidence rates and other factors?

NEA's Campaign

'Do the Mozzie Wipeout' campaign was launched in 2012.

Purpose

The campaign serves to encourage the community to participate in dengue prevention through actively checking and removing stagnant water in their homes by following the 5-step Mozzie wipeout.

5-Step Mozzie Wipeout

1. Change water in vases/bowls on alternate days.

2. Turn over all storage containers.

3. Remove water from flower pot plates on alternate days.

4. Cover bamboo holders when not in use.

5. Clear blockages and put Bti insecticides in roof gutters monthly(National Environment Agency, n.d).

Campaigns

1. Roadshows and Exhibits with live Aedes mosquitoes

2. Distribute campaign educational materials

3. TV commercials, radio and print ads

4. Facebook channel to engage with the public

5. Instagram contests

Parting Words

It seems like all factors have little or no correlation with the dengue rates. It is hard to pinpoint one significant factor that contributes to the incidence rates of dengue - the rates continue to fluctuate. We can then conclude that it is the combination of human demographics, man-made and environmental factors that create conditions perfect for Aedes mosquitoes to breed and grow.

We must also consider other factors that our quantitative analysis failed to address. Factors such as globalization are hard to quantify but play a significant role . With globalization, increased connectivity means that epidemics and outbreaks are more likely to occur. When they do, the spread is enormous and this applies for all types of diseases and outbreaks. It is premature to say that only one factor is responsible when all factors should be considered.

Recommendations

Overall, we can conclude that dengue occurs as a result of the interplay of various factors. Most of these factors are beyond our control and hence the only way to fight dengue is to prevent the breeding of vector mosquitos. Everyone has a to part to play in eradicating stagnant waters by doing the ‘5 step mozzie wipeout’ (Fig. 1).

Recommendation 1: Promote the dengue Vaccine

In 2017, Dengvaxia, a vaccination against dengue was approved for use by Singapore’s Health Sciences Authority. This vaccine is effective at reducing dengue by 60% and severe dengue by 84%. However, at this juncture, the vaccine is ineffective against the two most common dengue strains in Singapore (Salma, 2017). Once improvements are made to this vaccine, NEA and the Ministry of Health should encourage the public to get vaccinated to further reduce the chances of contracting dengue. Only through prevention can the spread of dengue truly be contained.

Recommendation 2: NEA to change social media strategy

Suggestion 1: Conduct data analysis to determine social media activities

With data analysis, NEA can determine certain periods with high dengue rates. This should prompt them into action - their social media efforts and complementary activities should be at its peak during those months (June). Leading up to June, NEA can conduct activities to promote dengue preventive measures. NEA should use these data to serve as a roadmap for their activities.

However, it is clear that quantitative analysis is not enough. To counter these limitations we conducted a short content analysis of NEA’s Facebook page via quintly.

Suggestion 2: Revamp their social media strategy

We can see that NEA’s posts garner very little reactions on facebook. However, only posts with participatory activities can gain up to more than 4000 reactions. This is indicative of what NEA should focus on - interactive activities from time to time to keep target audiences engaged. Their most recent social media activity invited users to screenshot a spinning gif into its correct place. NEA can extend this social media campaign by inviting users to take pictures of their preventive habits and hold a contest.

NEA’s various social media campaigns work as a stand alone and are carried out in the span of 4-5 weeks. Each individual campaign is not linked to one another. In order to create a better and more seamless social media campaign, NEA should an overarching social media strategy, with each social media campaign addressing different concerns under the same message strategy.

Suggestion 3: Create online social community to improve communication

Their response rates are also very low. In order to ensure that there is a two-way communication channel between Singaporeans and NEA, the social media team should be responsive. One method of doing so is to create a forum for residents to report potential dengue breeding spots dengue clusters around the neighbourhood. Creating a social community online can help to strengthen preventive actions and let others learn through leading by example.
