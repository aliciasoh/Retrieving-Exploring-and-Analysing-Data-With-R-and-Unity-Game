Human demographics - Gender and Age



From the graph above, we can see that the number of dengue cases for males is consistently higher than that of females. Secondary research has revealed that through extensive qualitative research, some have found that females are more health conscious and have greater care-seeking behaviour, which may translate into a lower number of infected females than males (Prasith, 2013).

We can also see that most of the dengue cases are contracted by individuals between the ages of 25 and 34 years old. There is no existing research in Singapore about why this age group is the most vulnerable - research from other countries also show that the the age range for vulnerability is inconsistent. However, this age group is the most active which can explain the higher than normal number of cases than the other age groups. Longer exposure periods to the external environment (e.g going to work) may result in a higher chance of contracting dengue.



Urban problems - Construction sites, Landfills



Mosquitoes often breed in places where water can collate, a prime spot for them to lay eggs. Hence this makes waste landfills an ideal location. In the graph above however, we can see that there seems to be little correlation between the total dengue incidence rates and the total waste landfill as the number of dengue cases spiked up in 2013, but remained the same for the total waste landfilled. Further, the number of dengue cases decreased from 2014 to 2015, but the total waste landfilled increased instead. One possible reason for this is that Semakau is located 8km from the south of Singapore which makes it too far away from civilians (National Environment Agency, 2015). Perhaps for a better analysis, the locations of each dustbin should be collected and compared with the incidence rates.

From the graph above, we can see that there is a slight correlation between the number of contracts awarded for construction projects and the dengue incidence rates in Singapore. Both the contracts awarded and the number of dengue cases increased from 2012 to 2013 and decreased from 2014 to 2015. This is supported by an article by Channel NewsAsia (Lee, 2015) that reports the high likelihood of mosquitos breeding in construction sites due to the conditions present at such areas. As such, it is evident that NEA needs to step up efforts in curbing mosquito breeding in stagnant waters at construction areas through consistent inspections and stringent enforcement of regulations put in place.

Environmental problems - Rainfall, Temperature, Humidity, Month



From the graph above, we can see that the dengue rates are somewhat determined by the interplay of two factors: the temperature and rainfall across the year. It is most likely that an inverse relationship (e.g rainfall high, temperature low) between the rainfall and temperature affects dengue rates. This is shown in June (low rainfall high temperatures) and July/ August (high rainfall, decreasing temperature), where the dengue rates are the highest.

From the data, we can conclude that rainfall and temperature correlates with dengue rates.
